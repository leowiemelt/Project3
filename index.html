Design Rationale

California's wildfire crisis has intensified dramatically over the years, driven by drought, rising temperatures, and expanded development in susceptible landscapes. This visualization includes interactive exploration of how fire activity has evolved over space and time across the state using NASA's MODIS Terra satellite and historical fire perimeter data.

Satellite tile overlay: Rather than abstracting fire locations into dots on a blank basemap, we use the NASA GIBS REST API (WMTS) to fetch actual MODIS satellite imagery tiles projected over California. This lets viewers see smoke plumes, burn scars, and active fire fronts in their real geographic context. A D3 SVG layer sits on top to render county boundaries and handle click interactions.

Time slider: Temporal change is the central story here. A static map can show accumulated fire history but not when or how rapidly conditions evolve. The slider lets viewers scrub day-by-day through any fire season, exposing seasonal patterns (peak activity: July–October) and year-over-year escalation. An animated playback mode runs the map forward automatically.

Layer toggle: Daytime thermal anomalies highlight active hotspots; nighttime detections capture smoldering fires after dark; true color provides atmospheric context including smoke plumes and terrain. Toggling between layers lets viewers examine the same event from multiple sensor perspectives.

County click + sidebar stats: Satellite imagery conveys location and extent but not magnitude. Pairing the map with an acreage bar chart (from the CAL FIRE CSV) grounds visual impressions in concrete numbers. Clicking a county surfaces localized statistics — total fires, total acres, largest single fire — enabling regional comparisons impossible in a static view.

Color and typography: A dark background with amber/orange accents reinforces the fire theme while maximizing contrast for the satellite imagery. Bebas Neue and Space Mono give the interface a data-instrument character appropriate to a scientific monitoring tool.

Data Transformations

CAL FIRE FRAP fire perimeters were filtered to California fires from 2000–2024 and aggregated in two ways: by year (total acres burned, for the bar chart) and by county (fire count, total acres, largest fire name and size, worst year). GIBS WMTS tiles are requested at 250 m/pixel resolution using the EPSG:4326 geographic projection; the date parameter updates on every slider change. Daily cloud cover can occasionally obscure imagery — comparing nearby dates usually resolves this.
