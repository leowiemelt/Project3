    Design Rationale

    California's wildfire crisis has intensified dramatically over the years, driven by drought, rising temperatures, and real estate development in fire-prone landscapes. This visualization provides interactive exploration of how fire activity evolved across the state in 2020, using the MODIS Terra satellite (via the GIBS WMTS API).

    Satellite tile overlay: Rather than abstracting fire locations into dots on a blank basemap, we fetch actual MODIS satellite imagery tiles directly from the NASA GIBS REST API (WMTS, EPSG:4326 projection) and draw them onto an HTML canvas element. D3's geoEquirectangular projection is used throughout — this matches the plate carrée coordinate system that GIBS tiles use internally, ensuring tiles align correctly with the SVG county overlay without any reprojection. A D3 SVG layer sits on top of the canvas for county boundaries and interaction targets.

    Two-layer compositing: The fire detection layers (Active Fires Day and Night) are served by GIBS as PNG tiles with a transparent background — only fire hotspot pixels carry color. When either is selected, the map renders the MODIS TrueColor basemap first, then composites the thermal anomaly PNG on top, making hotspots visible over real satellite imagery. The opacity slider controls how strongly the fire layer appears relative to the basemap. When TrueColor alone is selected, only one tile pass is needed.

    Time slider with playback: Temporal change is the central part of the dynamic visualization. A static map can show the overall pattern of fire-prone regions but cannot reveal when fires occur or how rapidly conditions escalate season by season. The slider steps through monthly dates from 2020, updating the GIBS tile request on each change. Animated playback lets the map run forward automatically, making the seasonal pulse of fire activity (peaking July–October each year) immediately visible. Clicking a bar in the year chart jumps the slider directly to that year.

    County click + sidebar statistics: Satellite imagery is powerful for conveying spatial extent and atmospheric context, but it does not convey magnitude. Pairing the map with a statewide bar chart and county-level statistics grounds the visual impressions in concrete numbers. Clicking a county surfaces its total fire count, total acres burned, largest single fire, and worst year — enabling regional comparisons that would be impossible in any static view. Hovering a county shows its name in the controls bar without requiring a full click.

    Visual design choices: A dark background maximizes the perceived brightness and contrast of the MODIS satellite imagery. Orange accents are used consistently across interactive elements — active buttons, selected counties, bar chart highlights, and the date display — creating a coherent visual language. Bebas Neue (display) and Space Mono (data labels) give the interface a scientific monitoring instrument character without sacrificing readability.
